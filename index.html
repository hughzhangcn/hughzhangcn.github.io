I am Professor in the Division of Biostatistics and Informatics in the Department of Preventive Medicine at Northwestern University Feinberg School of Medicine. I received my PhD degree in Statistics in 2010 from University of Rochester and immediately joined St. Jude Children's Research Hopital firstly as Assistent Member and then promoted to Associate Member in the Department of Biostatistics. Then I joined Northwestern University as professor in 2019.

My research these days mostly focuses on clinical trial design, longitudinal discrete data with missingness, and high resolution microscope image analysis. I received a NIA P30 as the Director of Data Management and Statistics Core of Northwestern Alzheimer's Disease Research Center, and NCI P50 as the director of Biostatistics and Bioinformatics Core for the Brain Tumor SPORE.

I received the Teaching Excellent Award from Northwestern University in 2021, received JSM Student Paper Award in 2009, ENAR Best Student Paper Award in 2010.

I was the president of American Statistical Association West Tennessee Chapter in 2016-2019.
